# Titanic Statistical Analysis

A statistical analysis of Titanic passenger data prepared as a university project at the University of Warsaw.

## Project overview

The aim of the project was to analyse selected characteristics of Titanic passengers and examine how factors such as sex, passenger class and age were associated with survival.

The project includes data cleaning, descriptive statistics, statistical hypothesis testing and visualisation of the results.

## Analysis scope

- data cleaning and handling missing values,
- descriptive statistics for quantitative and categorical variables,
- analysis of passenger age by class,
- comparison of survival rates by sex and passenger class,
- chi-square test of independence,
- Wilson confidence interval for the survival proportion,
- Welch's two-sample t-test,
- assessment of age distribution using a QQ plot,
- Kolmogorov–Smirnov test,
- preparation of charts and presentation of conclusions.

## Visualisations

The project includes:

- boxplot of passenger age by class,
- histogram of passenger age,
- heatmap of survival rates by sex and passenger class,
- QQ plot,
- comparison of the empirical and fitted distribution functions.
